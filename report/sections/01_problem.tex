\section{Problem Statement}
% Source: proposal Section 1. Expand from notes.
Modern retail inventory management relies on manual shelf audits, barcode scans, and periodic
stock-taking, which are labor-intensive, error-prone, and lack real-time visibility. Out-of-stock
events cost retailers an estimated 4\% of annual revenue while overstock ties up capital. Dense
retail shelves---hundreds of occluded, visually similar products per image---are among the hardest
object-detection settings, and off-the-shelf models trained on COCO/ImageNet do not transfer well.
Finally, actionable inventory insights typically require technical staff to write SQL, creating a
bottleneck for non-technical stakeholders. This project addresses these problems with an
end-to-end, AI-powered detection-to-insight pipeline.
