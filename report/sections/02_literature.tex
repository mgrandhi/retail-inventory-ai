\section{Literature Review}
% Source: proposal Section 3.
\subsection{Object Detection in Dense Retail Environments}
Goldman et al.~\cite{goldman2019sku110k} introduced SKU-110K with a RetinaNet-based architecture,
soft-IoU loss, and an EM-Merger stage for extreme density. Transformer detectors
\cite{carion2020detr,zhu2021deformabledetr} replaced NMS with learned bipartite matching. YOLOv8
\cite{jocher2023yolov8}, anchor-free with an improved backbone, serves as our detection backbone.

\subsection{Fine-Grained Visual Classification}
Wei et al.~\cite{wei2019rpc} formalized fine-grained retail recognition via the RPC dataset
(200 categories). Standard backbones (ResNet~\cite{he2016resnet}) require domain-specific
augmentation and attention-based features.

\subsection{Vision-Language Models}
CLIP~\cite{radford2021clip} learns joint image-text embeddings enabling zero-shot retrieval;
LLaVA~\cite{liu2023llava} adds visual instruction tuning for structured extraction of brand,
category, and visible text---suited to auto-labeling.

\subsection{LLM-Powered Text-to-SQL and Security}
Schema-aware NL2SQL~\cite{lin2020bridge} and LLM orchestration frameworks enable parameterized SQL.
Indirect prompt injection~\cite{greshake2023injection} motivates OWASP-aligned guardrails at the
input, query-generation, and output layers.
